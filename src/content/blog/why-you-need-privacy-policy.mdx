If you run a website, a SaaS app, or an e-commerce store, you've probably heard you need a privacy policy. But why exactly? And do you really need one if you're a small operation?

## Short Answer: Yes, You Probably Do

Any website that collects **personal data** from visitors needs a privacy policy. "Personal data" is broad — it includes email addresses, names, IP addresses, cookies, analytics data, and more.

## The Legal Landscape

Multiple regulations around the world require privacy policies:

- **GDPR (EU/EEA):** Applies if you have any visitors from the European Union
- **CCPA/CPRA (California):** Applies if you have California residents visiting your site
- **CalOPPA:** Requires any website collecting PII from California residents to post a privacy policy
- **COPPA (US):** Applies if you collect data from children under 13
- **LGPD (Brazil):** Brazil's comprehensive data protection law
- **PIPEDA (Canada):** Canada's private-sector privacy law

## What a Good Privacy Policy Includes

A compliant privacy policy should cover:

1. **What data you collect** — contact info, analytics, payment data, etc.
2. **How you collect it** — forms, cookies, third-party services
3. **Why you collect it** — legitimate interest, consent, contractual necessity
4. **Who you share it with** — processors, third-party services
5. **User rights** — access, deletion, portability, opt-out
6. **Contact information** — how users can reach you
