Mobile apps often collect more types of data than websites. Here's what you need to know when creating a privacy policy for your app.

## App Store Requirements

Both **Apple App Store** and **Google Play Store** require apps to have a privacy policy if they collect data.

- **Apple:** Requires a link to your privacy policy during app submission
- **Google:** Requires a Privacy Policy link in your store listing and within the app

## Common Data Types in Mobile Apps

Mobile apps typically collect:

1. **Device identifiers** — IDFA, Android Advertising ID
2. **Location data** — GPS, coarse location
3. **Contacts and media** — with user permission
4. **Analytics** — usage patterns, crash reports
5. **Push notification tokens** — for messaging
6. **Payment data** — via in-app purchases or SDKs

## Third-Party SDKs

Most apps integrate SDKs that process data:

- **Analytics:** Firebase, Mixpanel, Amplitude
- **Crash reporting:** Sentry, Crashlytics
- **Advertising:** AdMob, Unity Ads
- **Payments:** RevenueCat, Stripe
- **Social:** Facebook SDK, Google Sign-In

Each SDK's data handling must be disclosed in your policy.

## Mobile-Specific Considerations

- **Transparency:** Describe data collection upfront with a consent dialog
- **Permissions:** Explain why you need each permission at time of request
- **Data retention:** Device data may persist even after app deletion
- **Children:** COPPA applies if your app targets children under 13
